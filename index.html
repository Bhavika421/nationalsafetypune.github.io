Audits

Fire Safety Audits

Fire Safety Audits as per Maharashtra Fire Prevention and Life Safety Measures Rules, 2009, Government, National Building Code 2016 & International Standards like NFPA.

Occupational Safety & Health Audits

Occupational Safety & Health Audits as per 'Indian Standard – 14489:1998, ISO: 4500, NBC 2016 and other national and international standard applicable to each particular industry.

Hazard Identification/Analysis

Hazard Identification & Risk Analysis Preparation & Review & Identification of Significant Risk and Suggest remedial measures to reduce the Risk as Low As Practicable to Prevent the Incidents in the Organisation.

Contractor Safety Audit

  • I. The Site-Specific Accident Prevention Plan
  • II. Potential hazards in assigned work areas (HIRA)
  • III. Methods to mitigate anticipated hazards
  • IV. Proper wear of required personal protective equipments
  • V. Emergency response procedures

Electrical Safety Audit

  • I. Review of the electrical hazards identification and risk assessment.
  • II. Review of the earthing and lightning protection system implemented in plant to ensure the equipment and human safety.
  • III. To check earth resistance of earth pits and sockets on sampling basis.
  • IV. Review of EPM (Electrical Preventive Maintenance) programme in the facility Review of the shutdown procedure, work permits, lock-out tags etc.
  • V. To check the hotspot in the electrical installation and equipment by using thermal imager.
  • VI. Review of the electrical distribution network & system of the plant emphasizing on adequacy and functionality of protection devices.
  • VII. Review of the electrical documents like register of electrical installations, single line diagrams, test records (Transformer oil test, Insulation Resistance test and Earth Resistance tests) and data sheets of critical electrical installations.
  • VIII. Review of the availability & reliability of emergency systems such as DG set, UPS, battery etc.
  • IX. Review of the records of training and competency of employee and contractor performing the electrical work.
  • X. Review of the awareness of employee and contractors related to electrical safety, electrical hazard at workplace and the precautions to be taken.
  • XI. Identify the training needs of the plant employee from the point of view of electrical safety.
  • XII. Physical verification of warning signage & labelling on all electrical distribution panels, transformer and switchgear.
  • XIII. Review of fire system protection

Chemical Safety Audit

  • I. Organisation & Administration
  • II. Chemical Hazard Identification & its Contrli
  • III. Fire Hazards & Industrial Hygiene
  • IV. Employee participation, Motivation & Training
  • V. Emergency response Planning & Procedures

Environmental Safety Audit

  • I. Environmental Management System based on the ISO 14001:2015 standard requirements.
  • II. Environmental impact assessment
